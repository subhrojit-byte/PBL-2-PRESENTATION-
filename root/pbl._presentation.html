Introduction

Shell companies, though lawful in their formation, have become central enablers of global financial and cybercrimes. Their opaque structures, lack of real operations, and transnational reach make it challenging for authorities and financial institutions to detect misuse, trace ultimate ownership, and prevent illegal activities such as money laundering, tax evasion, and the laundering of cybercrime proceeds.

This has led to multi-billion dollar scandals and significant regulatory shortcomings worldwide.

Original Idea

Initial Goal

Detect shell companies in real financial systems, as they are commonly linked to fraud, money laundering, and tax evasion.

Challenge

Research is limited due to lack of accessible real-world datasets containing verified shell company behavior.

Evolution

Project direction evolved to building a realistic financial simulator that generates synthetic companies, directors, addresses, transactions, and risk scores. The simulator provides a controlled dataset to support future work in shell company detection, financial analytics, and machine learning-based fraud identification.

Research Gaps & Insights

Most studies rely on confidential or restricted financial datasets, limiting academic experimentation.

Existing work focuses on detection algorithms and AML indicators, but not on open simulation environments for testing.

Limited availability of labeled shell company behavior data for training analytical or ML models.

Research highlights the importance of transaction patterns, ownership networks, and offshore jurisdictions as key risk signals.

From these insights, this project develops a synthetic shell company simulator to generate structured, analyzable datasets for future detection research.

Problem Statement

Shell companies are widely used in money laundering, tax evasion, and financial fraud.

Real financial data on shell companies is confidential, making it difficult to study fraud patterns or train detection models.

There is no realistic academic platform to simulate shell company behavior and test detection techniques safely.

Objectives

01

Design a realistic shell company simulation system based on patterns from financial crime case studies and research.

02

Generate synthetic but believable financial data including companies, directors, addresses, and transactions.

03

Model risk indicators and scoring logic to identify suspicious shell-like behavior.

04

Create a platform for future fraud detection research or ML training without using confidential real-world data.

Proposed Methodology

Overall System Flow

Literature-based risk indicators for shell companies identified
Synthetic data generation for companies, directors, addresses, transactions
Rule-based risk scoring applied on generated entities
Dashboard visualization for analysis and research use

Synthetic Data Generation

Separate generators for Company, Director, Address, Transaction
Legit vs Shell behavior modeled differently
Offshore jurisdictions and abnormal patterns injected
Ensures realism without using confidential data

Risk Detection Logic

Indicators derived from Panama Papers & FATF research

Low employees but high transactions
Offshore registration
Shared directors or addresses
Round figure transactions
Weighted scoring to compute final risk score

Outcome

1.Functional Shell Company Simulator Developed

  • Generates realistic legitimate and shell companies
  • Includes transactions, directors, addresses, and risk scores
  • Mimics patterns seen in offshore financial investigations

2.Risk-based Detection Logic Implemented

  • Rule-based scoring using employee vs revenue mismatch
  • Offshore incorporation patterns detection
  • Structured round-value transactions analysis
  • Produces quantifiable suspicion score → Foundation for future ML detection

3.Research-Driven Synthetic Dataset Created

  • Solves real-world data unavailability problem
  • Enables safe experimentation without legal/privacy issues
  • Useful for future AML or fraud-detection research

4.Working End-to-End System Built

  • Backend simulator + database + frontend dashboard
  • Users can run simulation and explore generated financial data
  • Demonstrates practical feasibility of the concept
